chapter 9 Reapted Game

什么是repeated games？

repeated game is a model about such long-term relationships

重复博弈包含有限重复博弈和无限重复博弈；

formal model

Stage game

在repeated game中，player play the same strategic game G repeatly which is called stage game

$ G=(N,(A_i),(u_i))$满足假设:

  • Player: $N={1,\cdots,n}$
  • Action: $a_i\in A_i$(finite or compact&covex in $\R^k$)
  • Payoff: $u_i:A\to \R$

History

  • 某一特定策略组合的发生（包含在整个的game之中的）

  • a period t history $h_t=(a_1,\cdots,a_{t-1})\in H_t=A^{t-1}$ is a sequence of the past action profiles at the beginning of period t.

  • the initial history is $H_1={0}$by convention

  • $H=\bigcup^\infty_{t=1}H_t$ is the set of all such histories

  • subhistory：在一个history中的子集合，所包含的notes is no more than whole history

Strategy and payoff

Player i’s (pure) strategy $s_i\in S_i$ is a mapping from H to $A_i$

Mean that the past time(history) will decisive what strategy.

Payoff: a strategy profile $s\in S$ generates a sequence of action profiles $(a_1,a_2,\cdots)\in A^\infty$. Player i’s discounnted average payoff given s is $$ V_i(s):=(1-\delta)\sum_{t=1}^\infty \delta^{t-1}g_i(a_t) $$ Notation: $\delta \in [0,1)$ is a discount factor.

Another definition of repeated game: this extensive game with simultaneous moves.

we use subgame perfect equilibrium.

Further to difinite repeated game :play stage game T times, with the outcome of all preceding stages（在前的）在下一阶段博弈开始之前；

proposition：

  • 若阶段博弈有唯一一个NE，在有限次数下，重复博弈有唯一一个SPE；
  • 若G是一个静态博弈有多个NE，则子博弈在t<T下不会是NE

Minmax Payoff

$\underline v_{i}=min_{a_{-i}\in A_{-i}}max_{a_i\in A_i}g_i(a)$

intuitively $\underline v_i$is the payoff that player i can secure when player i knows the other players’ actions.

minmax payoff serves as a lower bound on equilibrium payoffs in repeated games.

static game

  • time is absent

Extensive Form games

  • capture time
  • With the introduction of time, players can adopt strategies contingent on the moves of others.

Key ideas

  • Game trees: graphical representations
  • Histories: sequences of moves
  • Strategies: complete plans of actions
  • Subgame Perfect Equilbrium: strengthens (refines) Nash equilibrium concept

Theorem

In games with perfect information and finite actions, backwards induction identifies the set of subgame-perfect equilibria of the game exactly.

In game theory, a trigger strategy is any of a class of strategies employed in a repeated non-cooperative game.（重复非合作博弈） A player using a trigger strategy initially cooperates but punishes the opponent if a certain level of defection (i.e., the trigger) is observed.
